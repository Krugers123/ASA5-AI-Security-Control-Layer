# ASA 4 To ASA 5

## Shared Core

ASA 4 and ASA 5 emerge from the same architectural core:

- trajectory integrity
- drift detection
- threshold interpretation
- external reasoning over time

The shift is not a replacement of one system by another.
It is an operational evolution.

## ASA 4

ASA 4 is the research-development layer.

Its role is to:

- study long-horizon Human-AI interaction
- map semantic drift
- observe instability before visible failure
- develop interpretive and trajectory-oriented logic

ASA 4 is where the architecture is explored and refined.

## ASA 5

ASA 5 is the production-security layer.

Its role is to:

- monitor runtime integrity
- classify pre-incident instability
- support operator escalation
- connect trajectory interpretation to security workflows

ASA 5 is where the architecture becomes operational.

## Operational Difference

In ASA 4, threshold interpretation helps explain instability.

In ASA 5, threshold interpretation also determines when instability becomes:

- `watch`
- `elevated`
- `pre-incident`
- `incident-active`

That is the shift from research to runtime.

## Summary

ASA 4 and ASA 5 are not separate worlds.

They are the same architecture under different operational conditions:

- ASA 4 asks what is happening in the trajectory and why
- ASA 5 asks when that condition becomes operationally significant and what must happen next
