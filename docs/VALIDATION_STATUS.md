# Validation Status

## Current Evidence

ASA5 Core V2 is an implemented and automated-test-covered local prototype. Current
evidence includes deterministic unit and integration tests, synthetic fixtures,
public-only dialogue pilots, trajectory playback, shadow analysis, and operator-
facing diagnostics.

## What This Supports

The available evidence supports claims that:

- the described local architecture has been implemented;
- the tested contracts behave deterministically for the covered fixtures;
- the evidence, inference, and governance boundaries are represented in code;
- the console can demonstrate the implemented operator workflow;
- TDB can preserve and distill public-source provenance under its stated rules.

## What This Does Not Yet Support

The available evidence does not establish:

- independently measured production effectiveness;
- calibrated real-world incident probability;
- generalization across models, domains, languages, or organizations;
- certified functional-safety performance;
- adversarial robustness under an agreed threat model;
- a production service-level objective;
- autonomous authority over an observed AI system.

## Required Next Validation Layers

1. frozen public-safe benchmark fixtures;
2. stable and negative-control trajectories;
3. false-positive and false-negative review;
4. independent reviewer reproduction;
5. domain-specific partner evaluation;
6. calibration only after sufficient representative evidence;
7. security and operational readiness review before deployment.
